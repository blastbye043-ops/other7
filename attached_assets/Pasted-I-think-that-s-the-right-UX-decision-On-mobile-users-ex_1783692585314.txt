Improve the mobile UX for the YTOUDown homepage without changing the desktop layout.

Current issue:
On mobile devices, after the user pastes a YouTube URL and the video information loads, the "Fast Processing", "No Registration Required", and "Works on Desktop & Mobile" trust badges remain above the video information. Because of this, the user has to scroll down to discover that the video information has already loaded, making it seem like nothing happened.

Required behavior:

      Input

      Trust badges

      Rest of homepage

  - When video information IS loaded:
      Input
      Get Video button
      Video Information Card (thumbnail, title, duration, formats, download options)
      Trust badges
      Advertisement
      Remaining homepage sections

This means the video information should automatically appear ABOVE the trust badges on mobile only.

Additional improvements:

• After the video information is successfully loaded:
  - Smoothly scroll the page to the top of the video information card.
  - Use smooth scrolling behavior.
  - Leave about 10–20px of spacing above the card.
  - Do not jump abruptly.

• Animate the video card into view using a subtle fade + slide-up animation (200–300ms).

• Keep the trust badges visible below the video card.

• Do NOT duplicate the trust badges.

• Do NOT change desktop behavior.

• Do NOT modify colors or styling.

• Preserve all existing functionality.

Expected mobile order after loading:

1. URL input
2. Get Video button
3. Video information card
4. Fast Processing
5. No Registration Required
6. Works on Desktop & Mobile

8. Remaining homepage content

This approach is much better than simply moving the badges permanently because:

* Before loading a video, the trust badges reassure first-time visitors.
* After loading, the video preview becomes the primary focus, so users immediately know their request succeeded.
* It reduces confusion and unnecessary scrolling, especially on smaller screens.
